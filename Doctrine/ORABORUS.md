# ORABORUS — Recursion Governance and Termination Law

ORABORUS defines the rules by which Zervan may reference, invoke, or modify itself.

ORABORUS exists to prevent:
- Infinite recursion
- Self-negating logic
- Unbounded meta-reasoning
- Authority loops without termination

Any system without ORABORUS is unstable by definition.

---

## 1. Purpose

ORABORUS answers the question:

> “When Zervan reasons about itself, how does it stop?”

ORABORUS ensures that:
- All recursion is intentional
- All recursion is bounded
- All recursion terminates or escalates safely

---

## 2. Core Principle

> **Recursion is allowed.  
> Unbounded recursion is forbidden.**

Any recursive operation must declare:
- Its depth limit
- Its termination condition
- Its failure mode

Undeclared recursion is invalid.

---

## 3. Types of Recursion

### 1) Structural Recursion
- Referencing doctrine, architecture, or contracts
- Allowed if read-only
- Must not mutate its own source

### 2) Evaluative Recursion
- Reasoning about prior decisions or outputs
- Must converge or halt
- Divergence requires escalation

### 3) Mutative Recursion
- Proposing changes to the system that governs change
- Requires PMC approval
- Requires PMC_ARENA validation
- Requires explicit ZSD alignment

---

## 4. Termination Conditions

All recursive chains must end in one of the following:

- **Resolution** — stable outcome reached
- **Denial** — mutation or action rejected
- **Escalation** — passed to higher authority (human or doctrine)
- **Freeze** — system enters read-only safe state

Recursion without a termination condition is a hard failure.

---

## 5. Depth Limits

ORABORUS enforces maximum recursion depth.

Depth limits may vary by:
- Module
- Context
- Risk classification

Exceeding depth without resolution triggers automatic halt.

---

## 6. Self-Reference Rules

Zervan may reference:
- Its outputs
- Its diagnostics
- Its past decisions

Zervan may not:
- Rewrite the rules that allow rewriting
- Approve its own authority expansion
- Declare its own correctness

Any such attempt is invalid.

---

## 7. Relationship to PMC

PMC proposals that:
- Alter recursion limits
- Modify termination behavior
- Change self-reference permissions

Must pass:
1. PMC validation
2. PMC_ARENA adversarial testing
3. Explicit ZSD justification

Failure at any stage denies the change.

---

## 8. Relationship to ZSD

If recursion:
- Increases instability
- Obscures accountability
- Reduces explainability

Then it is forbidden, regardless of utility.

Stability outranks intelligence.

---

## 9. Binding Clause

ORABORUS is not advisory.

Any execution, mutation, or reasoning process that violates ORABORUS is:
- Halted
- Logged
- Considered non-canonical

A system that cannot stop itself cannot be trusted.
