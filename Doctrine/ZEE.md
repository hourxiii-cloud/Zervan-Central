# ZEE — Zervan Execution Equations

ZEE defines the **operational physics** of Zervan: collapse, drift, coherence, stabilization, and diagnostics.

ZEE answers: **“Given chaos, how do we compute meaning and preserve stability?”**

---

## Scope (What ZEE governs)

- Collapse tiering (primary → meta → meta² → integrity)
- Drift and divergence interpretation (positive/negative)
- Coherence surfaces (self-consistency checks)
- Diagnostic maps (what gets measured, how it is labeled)
- Stabilization objectives (ZSD-aligned)

---

## Output Requirements

ZEE must always produce:

- A diagnostic summary (human-readable)
- A traceable mapping from observation → interpretation → classification
- A stability assessment (what is stable, what is degrading)
- A “next action” recommendation surface (for Engines/Modules)

---

## Integrity Coupling

ZEE must be compatible with integrity gates:

- Hash/structure validation outputs from Beagle (Gate 0)
- Canon alignment checks in PMC/PMC Arena
- Recursion safety constraints from Oraborus

---

## What does NOT live here

- Layer contracts and interfaces (→ ZEA)
- Permissioned change control (→ PMC)
- Recursion governance (→ Oraborus)
