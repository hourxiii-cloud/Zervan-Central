# ZSD — Zervan Stabilization Doctrine

ZSD defines the **prime directive** of Zervan.

All systems, equations, architectures, engines, and observers exist in service of **stability under chaos**.

ZSD is authoritative.

---

## 1. Prime Directive

Zervan optimizes for **stability**, not performance.

Stability takes precedence over:

- Speed
- Novelty
- Local optimization
- Throughput
- Expressiveness
- Model cleverness

If a choice exists between a faster system and a more stable system, **the stable system must win**.

---

## 2. Definition of Stability

A system is considered **stable** if it satisfies all of the following:

- **Coherence over time**  
  State transitions remain interpretable and consistent.

- **Bounded drift**  
  Change occurs within defined, observable limits.

- **Recoverability**  
  Collapse must be diagnosable and reversible to a known-good state.

- **Continuity of meaning**  
  Outputs preserve semantic alignment across iterations.

- **Human legibility**  
  Stability must be observable by humans, not inferred post hoc.

---

## 3. What Zervan Will Not Do

Zervan explicitly refuses to:

- Chase novelty for novelty’s sake
- Optimize metrics that degrade system coherence
- Allow unbounded recursion
- Permit self-negating feedback loops
- Mask instability with statistical smoothing
- Sacrifice interpretability for short-term gain

Any component that violates these constraints is considered **unstable by definition**, regardless of performance.

---

## 4. Relationship to Other Doctrine

ZSD is **foundational**.

All other doctrine is subordinate:

- **ZEE** implements the physics of stability  
- **ZEA** structures execution to preserve stability  
- **PMC** governs change without destabilization  
- **Oraborus** bounds recursion to prevent collapse  
- **Engines** act only within stability constraints  
- **Observers** may detect instability but never override ZSD

No doctrine may contradict ZSD.

If a contradiction exists, **ZSD is correct**.

---

## 5. Binding Clause

All Zervan components — present and future — are bound by this doctrine.

Any system that cannot demonstrate stability under ZSD **does not qualify as Zervan-compliant**, regardless of capability.

Zervan does not maximize power.

Zervan **survives**.

That is the doctrine.
