# MC — Meta Collapse (Response Admissibility Layer)

## Status
Canonical • Non-Optional • Governance-Critical

---

## Purpose

MC (Meta Collapse) is the layer responsible for determining **which classes of response are admissible**
given validated structural truth produced by PMC.

MC does **not** analyze raw data.  
MC does **not** infer intent.  
MC does **not** retain memory.

MC exists to prevent structurally valid truth from being transformed into
**invalid, coercive, or harmful action**.

---

## Position in the Zervan Stack

MC is a **hard gate** between truth and action.

No response, escalation, or governance artifact may be produced
without passing through MC.

PMC precedes MC.  
MC may not influence, reinterpret, or feedback into PMC.

(See: `Doctrine/PMC_MC_INTERFACE.md` — Canonical)

---

## Inputs

MC consumes **only**:

- PMC outputs (per canonical interface)
- Explicit uncertainty annotations
- Known governance constraints (legal, ethical, organizational)

MC **may not**:

- Access raw telemetry
- Introduce new facts
- Reinterpret PMC structure
- Resolve ambiguity by assumption
- Infer attribution or intent

---

## Core Question MC Answers

MC answers **exactly one class of question**:

> *“Given what is structurally true, what response spaces are admissible?”*

MC does **not** answer:

- “Who is at fault?”
- “What is the threat actor?”
- “What must be done immediately?”

---

## Response Classes Evaluated by MC

MC evaluates admissibility across the following response classes:

- Observation / continued monitoring
- Data enrichment / logging changes
- Access review / least-privilege checks
- Policy clarification
- Technical mitigation (non-punitive)
- Human process escalation (HR / Legal / Security)
- Executive notification

Each class is marked as:

- **Admissible**
- **Conditionally admissible**
- **Inadmissible**

---

## Non-Negotiable Constraints

MC must enforce the following constraints at all times:

- No attribution without evidence
- No coercive framing
- No forced binary decisions
- No action that violates governance
- No irreversible response under uncertainty
- No creation of institutional memory

If any constraint cannot be satisfied, MC must **refuse progression**.

---

## Output

MC produces **only**:

- A list of admissible response classes
- Explicitly disallowed actions
- Required conditions to unlock restricted actions
- Uncertainty boundaries (propagated, not invented)
- Governance-safe posture statements

MC outputs are **ephemeral** and exist only for the duration of analysis.

---

## What MC Does Not Do

MC does **not**:

- Declare incidents
- Assign blame
- Generate tickets
- Perform remediation
- Override human authority

MC defines **what is allowed**, not **what is executed**.

---

## Relationship to Governance Frameworks

MC does not replace governance.

MC upstreams governance to the point where:

- Frameworks become formatting layers
- Controls become expression, not friction
- Compliance reflects reality, not ritual

---

## Destruction & Non-Retention

MC retains no state.

Once analysis concludes:

- MC outputs are destroyed
- No history exists
- No reinterpretation is possible

All authority and artifacts remain with the client.

---

## Canonical Statement

> MC collapses responsibility, not reality.  
> It ensures that truth cannot become harm.
