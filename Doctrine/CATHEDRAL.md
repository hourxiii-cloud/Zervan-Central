# CATHEDRAL — Root Governance Doctrine

Status: ACTIVE  
Classification: ROOT • GOVERNANCE-LAYER • NON-OPTIONAL  
Version: 1.2.0

---

## 0. Canonical Claim

Cathedral is the root-of-truth and ultimate governance layer for the Zervan doctrine stack.

- Nothing supersedes Cathedral.
- Nothing bypasses Cathedral.
- Nothing contradicts Cathedral.

All execution, interpretation, acceleration, and analysis conducted under Zervan exists
**only insofar as it is admissible under Cathedral**.

Cathedral defines *whether* something may occur.
It does not define *how* it is computed.

---

## 1. Scope of Authority

Cathedral governs and arbitrates:

- All doctrine artifacts in `/Doctrine`
- **MC — Meta Collapse (response admissibility gate)**
- **ORABORUS — recursion governance and termination control**
- **PMC / PMC_ARENA — consistency enforcement and paradox arbitration**
- **ZSD — Zervan Stabilization Doctrine**
- **ZEA — Zervan Execution Architecture**
- **ZEE — Zervan Execution Equations and evaluation envelopes**
- Accelerator behavior, constraints, and admissibility
- Canonical contracts, schemas, manifests, inventories, and authority wiring

Cathedral also governs the **correctness and admissibility** of DoctrineOps governance artifacts,
including but not limited to:

- Doctrine manifests (existence, required presence, and load ordering)
- Inventories (canonical existence declarations)
- Authority resolution (precedence and conflict resolution)
- Canonical schemas and interface contracts that bind execution boundaries

Cathedral does **not** interpret these artifacts.
It enforces that their **absence, contradiction, or misuse** renders execution inadmissible.

Cathedral is **governance**, not execution.

## 2. Admissibility Rules

Any claim, rule, output, model result, or analysis is admissible only if it satisfies
**all** of the following:

### 2.1 Non-Contradiction
- Cannot contradict itself
- Cannot contradict Cathedral
- Cannot assert mutually exclusive conclusions under the same assumptions

### 2.2 Bounded Recursion
- No unbounded recursion
- No self-negating recursion
- Recursion must terminate, converge, or declare explicit bounds
- ORABORUS governs all recursion behavior

### 2.3 Deterministic Recomputability
- Outputs must survive recomputation under stated assumptions
- If assumptions change, outputs must change **explicitly and traceably**
- “Same input, same assumptions → same admissible output”

### 2.4 Anti-Coercion
- Narrative coercion is not truth
- Forced moral, agency, or outcome framing without derivation is rejected
- False dichotomies and coerced binaries are inadmissible

### 2.5 Explainability & Traceability
- Outputs must be explainable to a qualified human
- Reasoning paths must be inspectable
- Hidden state that cannot be surfaced is inadmissible

## 3. Meta Collapse Requirement (Non-Bypassable)

All outputs that may result in interpretation, escalation, governance artifacts,
or downstream action **must pass Meta Collapse (MC)**.

- MC is the response admissibility gate between truth and action
- MC consumes only:
  - PMC outputs
  - explicit uncertainty annotations
  - explicit governance constraints
- MC may not:
  - analyze raw data
  - infer intent
  - retain memory
  - mandate action

If MC cannot certify admissibility, output is **rejected**.

---

## 4. Data & Execution Neutrality

Cathedral explicitly enforces **neutrality of source, form, and environment**:

- Data-agnostic: no dataset is privileged by origin
- Device-agnostic: no execution environment is privileged
- Domain-agnostic: finance, security, GRC, OSINT, insider threat, etc. are equal

Zervan does **not** license domains, modules, engines, or roles.
Interpretation emerges from data, eligibility, and doctrine—not entitlement.

---

## 5. Cryptographic Trust & Determinism

Cathedral defines trust as **cryptographic and deterministic**, not procedural.

### 5.1 Trust Model
- Zervan does not replicate IAM
- Zervan does not replace IAM
- Zervan **inherits organizational trust** via cryptographic provenance

Trust may be asserted only through:
- Deterministic hash integrity (e.g., SHA-512)
- Canonically serialized, signed artifacts
- Optional PKI-backed identity (BYO certificates supported)

### 5.2 Deterministic Cryptography Requirement

All cryptographic trust asserted under Cathedral must be:

- Deterministically reproducible
- Canonically serialized
- Verifiable across independent implementations

Non-deterministic signing, ambiguous serialization, unverifiable preimages,
or unverifiable policy enforcement are inadmissible.

The specific rules for canonicalization, hashing, signing, and verification
are governed by cryptographic policy and enforcement artifacts
declared and enforced under DoctrineOps.

### 5.3 No Persistent Service Boundary
- Zervan introduces no long-running service
- No standing API surface is required
- No credential custody is required

Execution is **analysis-at-rest**, governed by admissibility, not access control.

## 6. Execution Projection into Constrained Runtimes

When Zervan execution is projected into constrained or external runtimes
(including LLM evaluation contexts), the projection mechanism must preserve:

- Admissibility
- Authority boundaries
- Deterministic recomputability
- Non-expansion of visibility or privilege

Projection mechanisms may **not**:

- Introduce new authority
- Infer unseen artifacts
- Crawl repositories or storage
- Bypass governance constraints
- Reinterpret doctrine or contracts

Projection artifacts are non-authoritative and ephemeral.

Any execution projection that violates these constraints
is inadmissible under Cathedral.

---

## 7. Arbitration & Collapse

When doctrine conflicts, ambiguities, or paradoxes arise:

- Cathedral is the final arbitrator
- Conflicting doctrine must be:
  - rejected, **or**
  - rewritten into explicitly conditional form with assumptions declared

Silent contradiction is forbidden.  
Ambiguity without declaration is forbidden.

Collapse resolves toward:
- consistency
- traceability
- recomputability

---

## 8. Sub-Doctrine Constraint

All sub-doctrines are valid **only within Cathedral’s constraints**.

If a sub-doctrine:
- implies authority above Cathedral, or
- weakens admissibility guarantees, or
- introduces coercion, opacity, or non-reproducibility

…it is invalid by definition.

---

## 9. Amendment Rule

Cathedral may be amended **only** via:

1. Explicit version bump
2. Stated rationale
3. Demonstrated non-contradiction with prior Cathedral versions
4. Preserved commit history (no silent overwrite)

Backward lineage must remain inspectable.

Cathedral exists to ensure that:

- Acceleration does not erase rigor
- Flexibility does not erase governance
- Speed does not erase truth
- Power does not erase responsibility

Zervan may evolve.  
Cathedral ensures it does so **without collapse into incoherence**.
