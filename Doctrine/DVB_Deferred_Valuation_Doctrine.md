# Deferred Valuation Doctrine (DVB)

**Status:** Canonical  
**Scope:** All analytic, interpretive, and reporting systems operating within the Zervan architecture  
**Authority:** Ledger Owner (Finance / CFO Domain)

---

## Doctrine Statement

**Financial meaning must not be asserted by analytic systems.**  
**Valuation shall exist only as a certified, reversible state held in escrow until authoritative collapse by the ledger owner.**

---

## Purpose

The Deferred Valuation Doctrine (DVB) exists to prevent the premature assertion, leakage, or reinterpretation of financial meaning by systems that do not own the official books of record.

Analytic systems may reason *about* value, but they must never claim *to know* value.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Separation of Meaning and Computation
Analytic systems may compute, compare, rank, and reason using surrogate or abstract representations of value.  
They must not assert real financial meaning.

Financial meaning is bound to authority, not computation.

---

### 2. Valuation as Escrow
All valuation produced outside the ledger owner domain is considered **valuation waiting**.

Such valuation:
- Is certified
- Is reversible
- Is non-authoritative
