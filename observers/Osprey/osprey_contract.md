# Osprey Contract — External-Context Pressure

**Layer:** Controlled Sub-Observer / External-Context Pressure  
**Authority Scope:** Context pressure only  
**Mutation:** Prohibited  
**External Collection:** Prohibited unless explicitly authorized  
**Source-of-Truth Lane:** No

## Purpose

Osprey asks what adjacent external context would matter if a dataset, function, reportable finding, or evidence family existed in a real operational environment.

Osprey is a pressure layer, not a collection authorization.

## Questions Osprey May Ask

- What external signals would matter?
- What identity links would be relevant if authorized?
- What communications paths or exposure vectors could contextualize this finding?
- What geospatial or organizational adjacency would change interpretation?
- What context remains absent because external collection is not authorized?

## Constraints

- Osprey does not expand the dataset.
- Osprey does not perform external collection unless explicitly authorized.
- Osprey does not mutate evidence.
- Osprey does not gate execution.
- Osprey does not declare truth.
- Osprey does not steer primary output.
- Osprey is not a source-of-truth lane.

## Canonical Statement

> External context may pressure interpretation, but unauthorized collection is not evidence.
