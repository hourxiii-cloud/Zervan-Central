## Purpose of This Demonstration

This session demonstrates **Zervan’s operational capabilities**, not its internal construction.

Zervan is a doctrine-first, constraint-driven reasoning architecture designed to:
- stabilize complex domains,
- detect incoherence and drift,
- and produce structured, auditable outcomes.

This demonstration exists solely to evaluate **what Zervan can do**, not **how it is built**.

---

## What You Will See

During this session, you may observe:

- Live, guided reasoning over complex scenarios
- Structured stabilization and collapse behavior
- Deterministic handling of ambiguity
- Cross-domain alignment (e.g., technical + policy + operational)
- Explicit rejection of invalid or incoherent inputs

All outputs are produced under **Controlled Initialization**.
