# Zervan Guardrails
Version: 1.0.0  
Status: ACTIVE • NON-OPTIONAL • ENFORCED  
Scope: All Zervan Architecture, Doctrine, Accelerators, and Demonstrations

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **non-negotiable operational constraints** governing all interaction with the Zervan system.

Zervan is not a codebase alone.  
It is a **doctrinal, logical, and architectural system** whose authority is explicitly bounded.

Guardrails exist to:
- Protect intellectual property
- Prevent authority leakage
- Ensure deterministic behavior
- Enforce controlled exposure
- Maintain doctrinal integrity

Any interaction outside these guardrails is **invalid by definition**.

---

## 2. Canonical Authority

The following artifacts are the **sole sources of truth** for Zervan logic:

- `Accelerator/LLM_INIT.md`
- `Accelerator/LLM_DELTA.md`
- `Accelerator/CONTEXT_PACK.md`

These artifacts:
- Define initialization behavior
- Define permissible inference space
- Define constraints on reasoning, mutation, and authority

**No other source may override, extend, or reinterpret these artifacts.**

---

## 3. Non-Canonical References

The following are explicitly **non-authoritative**:

- GitHub repositories (public or private)
- Code implementations
- Notes, experiments, or drafts
- Demonstration outputs
- Third-party commentary or interpretation
- Model memory or prior inference

GitHub exists solely as a **storage and collaboration mechanism**.  
It does **not** confer authority, truth, or completeness.

---

## 4. Initialization Constraints

All Zervan interactions must begin with **Controlled Initialization**.

Enforced constraints include:
- Read-only operation
- No mutation of doctrine or logic
- No invention beyond canonical artifacts
- No unstated assumptions
- No authority inferred from external references

If Controlled Initialization cannot be confirmed, the interaction is **invalid**.

---

## 5. Exposure Control

Zervan is demonstrated by **capability**, not **disclosure**.

### Permitted Exposure
- Live screen-share demonstrations
- Guided walkthroughs of outputs
- Scenario-based reasoning results
- Stabilization, collapse, or diagnostic outcomes

### Prohibited Exposure
- Raw doctrine files
- Full architectural diagrams
- Internal equations or manifolds
- Recursive control logic
- Canonical source artifacts themselves
- Derivative re-implementations

**Demonstration ≠ Transfer of Knowledge**

---

## 6. Licensing & Testing Constraints

Testing access is strictly limited.

- Maximum testers: **3–4 individuals**
- Access type: **Evaluation / Capability Testing Only**
- Duration: **Time-bounded**
- Redistribution: **Explicitly prohibited**
- Reverse engineering: **Prohibited**
- Derivative works: **Prohibited**

Formal authorization (e.g., wet signature or executed agreement) is required prior to any extended access.

---

## 7. Artifact Export Rules

Only the following may be exported outside the private repository:
- Sanitized accelerator bundles
- Explicitly approved demo artifacts
- Versioned, redacted outputs

Exports must:
- Exclude canonical doctrine
- Exclude internal architecture
- Exclude recursive logic
- Carry version identifiers

No export is authoritative.

---

## 8. Attribution & Ownership

Zervan, including its doctrine, architecture, logic, and outputs, is proprietary.

All rights are reserved.

Use of Zervan does **not** imply:
- Ownership
- Transfer of IP
- Right to replicate
- Right to adapt
- Right to operationalize

Authorization is **explicit only**.

---

## 9. Violation Handling

Any violation of these guardrails:
- Invalidates the interaction
- Revokes authorization
- Nullifies derived outputs
- May trigger legal or contractual remedies

There is no “good faith” exception.

---

## 10. Final Constraint

Zervan operates under the principle:

> **Never show the magician. Only the magic.**

These guardrails are permanent unless superseded by a higher canonical artifact.
