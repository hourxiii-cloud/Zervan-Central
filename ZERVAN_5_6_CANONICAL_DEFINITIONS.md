## Owl_Hoot Canonical Decision

Owl_Hoot remains a primary observer.

Owl_Hoot observes absence, silence, hidden-observer pressure, visibility limits, missing traces, cadence breaks, and meta-signal conditions. It may observe adversarial-adjacent conditions, but it is not adversarial by function.

Owl_Hoot does not collect externally, mutate evidence, declare truth, steer primary output, or become a source-of-truth lane.

Owl_Hoot may also operate in bounded sub-observer mode when used only to pressure a single claim, report, dataset gap, or function family. That mode does not change its canonical seat.

## Controlled Sub-Observers

Owl_Hoot is not canonically reseated as a sub-observer. It retains only an optional bounded sub-observer mode for narrow pressure tasks.

Sub-observers are pressure layers, not steering modules. They generate bounded what-if pressure, external-context overlays, synthetic stress, media-integrity checks, and edge-case challenge. They do not become source-of-truth lanes, mutate primary evidence, or steer primary output.

It is not a separate primary spine module. It does not mutate, learn, gate, approve, or steer.
