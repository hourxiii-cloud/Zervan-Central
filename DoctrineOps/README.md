DoctrineOps — Truth & Control Plane

Status: Canonical
Layer: Governance / Control Plane
Scope: System-wide
Authority: Derived (see Authority Resolution)

⸻

Purpose

DoctrineOps is the truth and control plane of Zervan.

It governs:
	•	What is considered true
	•	What is allowed to change
	•	How change becomes canonical
	•	How contradictions, recursion, and drift are prevented

DoctrineOps does not execute analysis.
DoctrineOps does not generate outputs.
DoctrineOps defines the constraints under which all execution, interpretation, and mutation occur.

If Modules are organs and Engines are muscles, DoctrineOps is the spine and nervous system.

No downstream component may bypass DoctrineOps.

⸻

Authority Model

DoctrineOps does not self-authorize.

Its authority is derived exclusively through:
	•	Cathedral (root governance doctrine)
	•	DoctrineOps manifests (e.g., DOCTRINE_MANIFEST.md)
	•	Explicit doctrine files enumerated by those manifests

Repository location, file presence, or implementation status
do not confer authority.

If a conflict exists between:
	•	Any implementation or documentation, and
	•	Cathedral or a DoctrineOps manifest,

The implementation or documentation is invalid.

⸻

Canonical Responsibility

DoctrineOps is responsible for:
	•	Declaring which doctrine exists
	•	Declaring load order and precedence
	•	Enforcing non-contradiction
	•	Enforcing bounded recursion
	•	Enforcing deterministic recomputability
	•	Enforcing anti-coercion
	•	Governing canonical mutation

DoctrineOps does not:
	•	Decide outcomes
	•	Interpret data
	•	Assign blame
	•	Mandate action

It defines what is allowed to exist, not what is done.

⸻

Canonical Components (Governed by DoctrineOps)

The following components are canonical only insofar as they are declared by DoctrineOps manifests and remain admissible under Cathedral.

1) Doctrine

Purpose: Defines principles, vocabulary, and non-negotiable constraints.
Outputs: Stable invariants, shared definitions, doctrine documents.

Doctrine is executable truth:
if implementation contradicts doctrine, implementation is wrong.

⸻

2) ZEA — Zervan Execution Architecture

Purpose: Architectural constitution.
Defines:
	•	Layer boundaries (Observers / Engines / Modules)
	•	Allowed data flows
	•	Interface contracts
	•	No-bypass rules

ZEA governs structure, not behavior.

⸻

3) ZEE — Zervan Execution Equations

Purpose: Operational physics.
Defines:
	•	Collapse behavior
	•	Drift detection
	•	Coherence and degradation laws
	•	Diagnostic consistency surfaces

ZEE makes Zervan predictable under uncertainty.

⸻

4) PMC — Probabilistic Multiverse Computation

Purpose: Permissioned truth collapse and mutation governance.

PMC is the only lawful path for:
	•	Canonical change
	•	High-impact interpretation
	•	Promotion of conclusions into authority

PMC evaluates multiple plausible worlds and collapses toward
the most stable, coherent, and admissible truth.

⸻

5) PMC_ARENA — Adversarial Validation

Purpose: PMC vs PMC consistency enforcement.

PMC_ARENA exists to:
	•	Detect interpretive overreach
	•	Detect confidence masquerading as truth
	•	Prevent single-author drift
	•	Stress-test mutation logic

Consensus without stability justification is invalid.

⸻

6) ORABORUS — Recursion Governance

Purpose: Termination authority.

ORABORUS forbids:
	•	Unbounded recursion
	•	Self-negating recursion
	•	Authority-amplifying feedback loops

Recursion must converge, terminate, or explicitly declare bounds.

⸻

DoctrineOps Law (Single Sentence)

No change is canonical unless it is:
consistent with Doctrine,
permitted by PMC, and
recursion-safe under ORABORUS.

⸻

Downstream Enforcement

Observers
	•	May observe everything
	•	May mutate nothing
	•	May inject context, never authority

Engines
	•	Are the only lawful state changers
	•	Must obey DoctrineOps gates (PMC / ORABORUS)
	•	Cannot bypass Meta Collapse (MC)

Modules
	•	Interpret, report, and actuate only within contracts
	•	Do not define truth
	•	Do not train or mutate canon

⸻

Relationship to Meta Collapse (MC)

DoctrineOps governs truth and change.
MC governs response admissibility.

DoctrineOps determines what is true.
MC determines what is allowed to be said or done.

Neither may impersonate the other.

⸻

Final Constraint

DoctrineOps exists to ensure that:
	•	Acceleration does not erase rigor
	•	Flexibility does not erase governance
	•	Confidence does not become authority
	•	Power does not bypass truth

Zervan may evolve.
DoctrineOps ensures it does so without collapse into incoherence.
